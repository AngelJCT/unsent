# Unsend — Brand Foundation

*The symbol and vocabulary system. Lock this before content creation. Everything downstream — ads, onboarding, empty states, the share artifact — flies this flag or it doesn't ship.*

---

## 0. The north star

One sentence the whole brand exists to make culturally true:

**The person who doesn't send it is the one in control.**

The default story says sending the calm version is *losing* — swallowing pride, going soft, being fake. The brand's single cultural job is to author the opposite: restraint is dominance. The calmest person in the thread won. If a tactic, line, or asset doesn't ladder to that reframe, cut it.

Underneath it sits the only emotion the brand ever points at, and it's already true for everyone: **every person has sent a message they regret.** We never manufacture this wound. We name one that already exists. That is the entire moat (see §6).

---

## 1. The Mark — the stopped dots

Three dots, frozen at the moment before they become a sent message.

It carries two meanings at once, and both are on-brand:
- **The typing indicator, stopped** — the message that stayed a draft.
- **The ellipsis** — the thing left unsaid, trailing off on purpose.

It satisfies every requirement of a symbol that spreads on its own: a child can draw it, it's pre-loaded with universal emotional charge (everyone has watched those dots appear and vanish), it needs no caption, and it creates instant in-group recognition.

### The three rules

1. **It must read as *stopped*, never *loading*.** Loading dots are anxiety; stopped dots are control. Even weight, even spacing, no animation in static use. Where it animates, it animates *toward stillness* — dots appear, settle, hold. The mark has *decided*; that's the only thing separating it from an ordinary typing indicator.

2. **It lives in the same punctuation family as the period.** The name is "Unsend." — finality, not suspension. The signature motion (use sparingly, for hero moments only) is three dots resolving into a single period: the flood becoming one composed point. That animation is the entire thesis in under a second.

3. **It never explains itself in context.** A symbol with a caption is just a logo. Let it appear unexplained — app icon, end of an ad, a sticker — and let the *wondering* recruit. The mystery does the work.

### Spec

- File: `unsend-mark.svg`. Uses `currentColor`, so set the color per surface: `#1c1917` (stone-900, the existing theme color) on light, a warm off-white like `#fafaf9` on dark.
- Dot proportion and spacing are the craft — the ownability is in the balance, not in adding detail. Do not add detail. Simplicity is the brief.
- Clear space: at least one dot-diameter of empty space on all sides. The mark needs silence around it; that silence is part of the meaning.

### What the mark is NOT
- Not animated loading dots.
- Not a speech bubble, not an envelope, not a paper plane. Every messaging app uses those. The point is to look like *none of them.*
- Not paired with a tagline as a permanent lockup. The wordmark "Unsend." can sit beside it; a sentence cannot.

## 2. The Lexicon — words that recruit by being spoken

A movement people belong to has its own language, and *using the language is the act of joining*. Keep it small — a few words people actually repeat beat fifty clever ones nobody does. The whole vocabulary fits on this page on purpose.

**The test for every phrase:** could a user say it *about themselves, with a little pride*? If it only works in the brand's mouth and not in theirs, it doesn't recruit. Cut it. The real test is whether someone would put it in a bio.

| Phrase | What it does | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **The unsent** | The noun for the in-group. "I'm one of the people who didn't send it." | An identity, not a feature. Flatters the self the user wants to be: composed, above the bait, in control. |
| **Drafts, not damage** | The restraint-is-power reframe in three words. | The most repeatable line. Sticker, bio, end-card. |
| **The first draft of yourself** | Names the raw message as a *version* of you, not the real you. | "Don't send the first draft of yourself." Makes not-sending feel like loyalty to who you are, not self-censorship. The deep-identity line. |
| **Say less. Mean more.** | Restraint as potency, not suppression. | Use where the audience suspects calm = weak. |
| **Some things don't need a record.** | (Already live on site.) Names the artifact, never the feeling — pure placed dot. | Keep. It's the model for how all copy should work. |

**The discipline:** these recruit only if they stay clean. The moment they're crowded with explanation, they stop being claimable and become marketing. Show the words; never gloss them.

## 3. The Voice — the thing that fuses whisper and broadcast

This is the most load-bearing section. The system only works if the same intelligence whispers to one person at 2am *and* addresses the crowd on a billboard — recognizably one entity. When the broadcast voice and the product voice match, a user who joined the movement and then hits their crisis feels like the friend who recruited them just turned to face them personally. Most brands break here: the ads sound like marketing and the product sounds like software. This one can't.

**The voice is:**
- **Calm** — the most composed presence in any frame, always.
- **Knowing** — a half-step ahead of the reader, tells them a true thing they were avoiding.
- **Never cruel** — it reveals, it doesn't scold or shame.
- **Economical** — says less, means more. Compression is part of the voice; a long sentence is usually a tell that you're forcing.

**It reveals; it never instructs.** It tells the reader what's *true*, never what to *do*. "She'll read this as: I'm still counting." — not "don't send this, you'll regret it."

### Register tells (what it must never slip into)

| Wrong register | Sounds like | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Therapist / scold** | "Take a breath. Think before you send." | Remove the instruction, state the fact. |
| **Hype / marketing** | "The #1 AI tool to transform your texts!" | Remove the adjective, trust the noun. |
| **Software / feature** | "Our AI analyzes tone and rewrites your message." | Name the human moment instead; hide the mechanism. |

If a line scolds, hypes, or sounds like a feature, it's not the brand — regardless of how good it is otherwise.

## 4. Spread mechanics — how the mark travels

A symbol that sits on your app icon is a logo. A symbol that *moves* is a movement. For the mark to spread, the **not-send has to become postable.**

At the moment of restraint, the product hands the user something to claim — **not the message** (stored nowhere; that's the architecture and the honesty), but the *fact* of the unsent. The content-free Receipt, reframed: not a feature, a **movement artifact.** "Typed it. Didn't send it." + the stopped dots.

Content-free is the asset, not a limitation — the drama was never in the words, it's in the restraint, and the restraint is the flex. Every person who posts their unsent is flying the flag, and each post recruits the next person. That's the loop:

> **Broadcast** (the mark + the shared wound, in daylight) → recruits people into caring about restraint as identity → **Whisper** (the mirror, at the private threshold) → converts: they don't send it → **the not-send becomes postable** → feeds the broadcast.

The movement pre-loads the moment: a user who already half-believes not-sending is the win meets the mirror with a primed mind instead of a defensive one. Breadth lowers the wall depth would otherwise climb.

## 5. Where the mark and words actually appear

- **App icon:** the mark, unexplained.
- **Site / hero:** "Unsend." + one placed-dot line. Current live copy ("For everything you almost said," "Some things don't need a record.") is already correct — keep it.
- **Ad end-cards:** the mark + one lexicon phrase, nothing else.
- **The share artifact:** the postable unsent — stopped dots, content-free, claimable.
- **Empty states / micro-moments in the app:** the whisper voice, one true line at a time.
- **Stickers / bio assets:** "Drafts, not damage." / "The unsent." / the mark alone.

## 6. The differentiation thesis — why this beats the AI-wrappers

There are hundreds of apps that rewrite your text with an LLM. Competing as "an AI rewriter" puts you against writing tools, on features, where the only axes are model quality and price — and you lose that race to whoever's cheapest. The brand is the exit from that race.

Three things the wrappers structurally cannot copy, because they're positioning, not technology:

1. **You own a moment, not a mechanism.** The wrappers sell *rewriting.* You sell *the pause between feeling and sending* — a category of one. Anyone can add a rewrite button; nobody else is claiming the threshold.

2. **You sell an identity, not a utility.** A tool is something people use and forget. "The unsent" is something people *are.* The status of "I typed it and deleted it" is a flex no feature list can manufacture.

3. **You run a true movement in a category built on lies.** The growth-hacked competitors manufacture urgency, scarcity, and FOMO. Your entire engine runs on the one thing they don't have — a real, universal, slightly embarrassing human moment everyone already recognizes. Movements built on truth survive the day the audience gets skeptical. Built on tricks, they don't.

The mark and the lexicon are how all three become *visible* before a user has typed a single word.

## 7. The copy-audit rubric (carry into your agent)

Score any copy 1–5 on six axes. Never make copy louder, cleverer, or more explicit — make it quieter and more inferential.

1. **Dot vs. line** — does it place true data and let the reader infer (dot), or state the conclusion (line)? Reward the dot. *Fix:* delete the asserted conclusion, replace with the most specific true observation that makes it inevitable. Name the artifact (record, screenshot, timestamp), never the feeling.
2. **Gap calibration** — is the inference gap *narrow* (one conclusion available) or so wide nothing connects? Reward narrow. *Fix:* too wide → add one constraining detail; forcing → remove the last sentence and let the prior one imply it.
3. **Restraint-as-power** — is not-sending framed as winning/control, or as softening/swallowing pride? *Fix:* recast the calm choice as the dominant one.
4. **Wound, not feature** — does it sell the universal moment, or the mechanics (AI, rewriting, tone)? *Fix:* lead with the moment; let the mechanism be invisible or last.
5. **Claimable identity** — could a user say this about themselves with pride? *Fix:* convert observations-about-the-user into identities-the-user-claims.
6. **Voice unity** — same composed, knowing, never-cruel voice at every scale? *Fix:* if it scolds, state the fact; if it hypes, cut the adjective; if it sounds like software, name the human moment.

**Auto-fail (rewrite regardless of score):**
- Instructs the user to feel or do something. The brand reveals; it never instructs.
- Describes the user's message back as a verdict ("this is desperate/angry"). Reflect the *recipient's* reading, not a judgment.
- Manufactures a wound that isn't already true, or implies a false consequence. **Weight this heaviest** — it's the line between the brand and the manipulation it borrows from. Flag any rewrite where the agent was tempted to exaggerate to make a line land; that temptation is exactly where a well-meaning system drifts.
- Fake scarcity, fake urgency, or guilt.

**Calibration step:** run the agent on the live site copy first. "Some things don't need a record." should score near-perfect. If the agent can't see why, tune the rubric before trusting it on anything new.
